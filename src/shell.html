🎬 How to build a prompt
1Select your Library Assets — characters, locations, props you've defined. Their descriptions auto-inject into the prompt.
2Pick a Frame Size — the app auto-selects the right lens and depth of field for you.
3Describe the action or scene in the Subject field. Type @name to link a library asset inline.
4Adjust Lighting, Mood, and Quality as needed — or leave defaults. The prompt builds live on the right.
5Select your Platform at the bottom, copy the prompt, and paste into your AI image generator.

🎬 How to use Storyboard
1Add your Characters and Locations to the Library first — use @name to reference them in your story.
2Write your story in the text area. Each sentence becomes a panel beat. Mention characters and locations by name.
3Set your Platform, choose how many panels, and optionally add a Visual Style that travels to every panel.
4Click Generate Storyboard. The app assigns shots, infers compositions, and builds cinematic prompts for each panel.
5Review each panel's Composition block — edit it to adjust the spatial layout. The prompt updates live.
✦ Enable Smart Split in Settings for director-level shot assignments via Claude API. ✦ Spatial Context on your Location asset improves shot accuracy.

Automatic Backup

A scheduled server-side job (3×/day via cron) zips this app's program files and copies userdata/ (projects, images, AI analysis) as plain files into the folder below. The last 2 days of backups are kept; older ones are pruned automatically. This only configures where backups go — the cron job itself is set up once, outside the app (your admin/IT step, not something this panel can do).

Backup destination folder
Must be an absolute path, must exist or be creatable, and must be outside this app's own folder (so a code-level deletion or corruption can't take the backups with it).
